The eye is a powerful organ that allows humans to see and understand the visual world. It works by receiving light through the cornea, which helps focus the light toward the lens. The lens then adjusts its shape to focus the light onto the retina, a layer at the back of the eye that contains special cells called rods and cones. These cells convert light into electrical signals that travel through the optic nerve to the brain. The brain then processes these signals and forms the images we see. Because of this complex system, the eye can detect colors, shapes, movement, and light intensity, making vision one of the most important human senses. 👁️
